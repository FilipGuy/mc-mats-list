Support

Find answers to common questions below. If you are stuck on something not listed here, the app is straightforward enough that most things are self-explanatory.

🔍
Account
On the login screen, click "Create one" below the sign-in form. Pick a username of at least 3 characters and a password of at least 4. That is the whole process — no email needed.
Right now there is no password reset because the app does not collect your email. Your data is stored only in your browser. If you forget your password you would need to create a new account. We recommend using a password you will remember or saving it somewhere safe.
Yes. Each account stores its data separately under its own username key. You can have multiple accounts on the same browser and they will never see each other's lists. Just sign out when you are done.
Data & Storage
Everything is stored in your browser's local storage. Nothing is sent to any server. This means your data stays on your device and is completely private — but it also means it is tied to the specific browser on that device.
Yes. Clearing your browser's cookies and site data will wipe local storage, including your Materials data. If you want to keep your data safe, avoid clearing storage for the site where you host the app.
Since data is stored locally in each browser, opening the app on your phone will start with a fresh empty list even if you have data on your PC. The two devices do not sync automatically. This is a known limitation of the current version.
Features
In the add item row, the third input is for a tag name. Just type any word and it will be saved as a color-coded tag. Once a tag exists you can click it below the add form to filter items by that tag.
Grab the dotted handle on the left side of any item and drag it up or down. Drop it where you want it. This only works when the sort is set to "Manual order" — if you have a different sort active the dragged order won't persist.
Project deletion is not in the current version. If you want to clear a project just remove all its items. A delete option will be added in a future update.
The app auto-detects whether your image is light or dark and switches the text theme accordingly. If it still feels hard to read, open the background panel and increase the Dim slider — this adds a dark overlay that makes text pop against any image.
Hosting
The easiest way is Netlify — go to netlify.com, create a free account, and drag your HTML file onto the dashboard. You will get a free link like yoursite.netlify.app in under a minute. No server setup needed.
No — even when hosted online, the data still lives in each browser's local storage. Syncing across devices would require a backend database, which is not part of the current version.

Still stuck?

Most issues come down to browser storage or account setup. Re-read the relevant FAQ above — the answer is usually there. If you are building on top of this project, the code is fully readable and well-commented.

Built for students
who just need a list

Materials started as a simple idea — there was no clean, private, fast place to track what you need for a project. So we built one. No accounts linked to emails, no data sent anywhere, no ads.

💡

Why we built it

Most list apps are either too complex, tied to an account system that requires an email, or full of features you never use. Materials cuts all of that out. It is just you and your list.

🔒

Privacy first

Your data never leaves your browser. No server stores your lists. No analytics track what you add. What you write is yours and only yours.

⚡

Fast by design

The entire app is a single HTML file. No frameworks, no install, no loading screen. Open it and it is already running.

🎓

Made for students

Whether it is a school project, a parts list, or supplies for a hobby, Materials fits the way students actually think and work.

What we care about
